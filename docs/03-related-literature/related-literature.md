# Related Literature

REVIEW OF RELATED LITERATURE AND STUDIES
This chapter presents the review of related literature and studies relevant to the research entitled “Enhancement and Optimization of the Camarines Norte Expanded Provincial Government High School Educational Assistance Program (CNEPGHSEAP) Management System.” The literature and studies reviewed provide the legal, global, conceptual, and empirical foundations of the study. To ensure coherence and relevance, the discussion is organized thematically rather than by foreign and local classifications.

Related Literature
Legal Basis
Scholarship and educational assistance programs are grounded in legal and policy frameworks that promote equitable access to education and ensure accountability in the use of public funds. Education is internationally recognized as a fundamental human right, and governments are encouraged to establish financial assistance mechanisms that support learners, particularly those from disadvantaged backgrounds.
UNESCO (2021) emphasized that inclusive education financing systems are essential in achieving equitable access to education. Scholarship and educational assistance programs are identified as vital instruments in reducing educational inequality when managed transparently and efficiently. These principles require scholarship administrators to adopt systems that ensure fairness, traceability, and proper documentation.
In the Philippine context, local government units are mandated to support education through scholarship and educational assistance programs. Digital platforms used in scholarship administration align with these legal mandates by ensuring proper record-keeping, secure data handling, and transparent evaluation processes. ZoomGrants (2024), as an online scholarship management system, reflects these legal principles by providing structured workflows, applicant tracking, and audit-ready records, which support accountability and good governance.
Global Concern
The rising cost of education and widening socioeconomic disparities have made scholarship programs a global concern. Around the world, governments and institutions rely on educational assistance programs to ensure that students are not deprived of education due to financial constraints.
Studies have shown that effective scholarship administration significantly contributes to improved access to education. Abdul-Rahman (2024) reported that efficient management of scholarship funds in Indonesia enhanced beneficiary satisfaction and program effectiveness. Similarly, international research revealed that financial assistance programs improve student retention and academic persistence (Samvakti Journals, 2025).
With the advancement of digital technologies, scholarship administration has shifted toward online systems to address efficiency and transparency issues. ZoomGrants (2024) responds to these global concerns by automating scholarship processes, minimizing delays, and ensuring fair evaluation. These global trends highlight the necessity of optimizing scholarship management systems such as the CNEPGHSEAP Management System.
Scholarship Management Systems
Scholarship management systems are designed to streamline application processing, evaluation, monitoring, and reporting. Literature emphasizes that automation reduces administrative burden and improves data accuracy.
Al-Ayyubi and Maulana (2023) noted that web-based scholarship systems improve accessibility and information dissemination while reducing processing time. Comprehensive scholarship platforms allow institutions to track beneficiaries and generate reports efficiently. ZoomGrants (2024) exemplifies an integrated system that supports application intake, reviewer collaboration, and performance monitoring.
These features are relevant to the present study, which seeks to enhance and optimize the CNEPGHSEAP Management System.
Related Studies
Studies on Scholarship Program Evaluation
Empirical studies evaluating scholarship programs demonstrate their impact on educational access and student outcomes. Abdul-Rahman (2024) evaluated a national scholarship program in Indonesia and found that effective fund management improved program reaches and equity. Similarly, studies assessing the impact of scholarships on international students revealed positive relationships between financial aid and academic persistence (Samvakti Journals, 2025).
These studies emphasize the importance of efficient scholarship administration systems in achieving program objectives.


Studies on Online Scholarship Management Systems
Research on online scholarship systems highlights their effectiveness in improving administrative efficiency. Benolirao et al. (2024) developed the SMART-Scholar system, which enhanced tracking and monitoring of scholarship beneficiaries. A study conducted at Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges revealed that an online scholarship management system reduced processing time and improved record accuracy (Global Scientific Journal, 2023).
Fajardo (2024) further demonstrated that a data-driven scholarship system improved decision-making accuracy and equitable allocation of funds in Philippine local government units. These findings are directly related to the present study.

Region-Based Studies
Region-based studies show that scholarship systems are widely used across countries to address local educational needs. In Southeast Asia, digital scholarship systems support expanding student populations and government-funded education programs (Abdul-Rahman, 2024). African scholarship program evaluations also revealed that inefficiencies in administration negatively affect program delivery, underscoring the need for optimized systems.

Province-Based Studies
At the provincial level, scholarship programs are managed by local governments and educational institutions. Studies conducted in Philippine provinces indicate that online scholarship systems improve transparency, monitoring, and beneficiary satisfaction (Global Scientific Journal, 2023). The online scholarship system implemented at Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges serves as a relevant provincial reference for the enhancement of the CNEPGHSEAP Management System.

Synthesis and Correlativity of the Reviewed Literature and Studies
A synthesis of the reviewed literature and studies reveals a strong correlation between effective scholarship management systems and program success. Automated systems are consistently associated with faster processing, improved transparency, and higher stakeholder satisfaction (Al-Ayyubi & Maulana, 2023; ZoomGrants, 2024). Moreover, studies show a positive relationship between efficient scholarship administration and student academic persistence and achievement (Samvakti Journals, 2025).
These correlations support the need to enhance and optimize the CNEPGHSEAP Management System to improve service delivery and educational outcomes.
